Climate-Resilient Agriculture: Strategies for a Changing World

Authors: Author Name¹*, Co-author Name²
¹[Department, University]   ²[Department, University]
Article ID: DA-2026-001   |   Pages: 1–5

Abstract: Climate change is posing a serious threat to global agriculture, affecting crop production, livestock, fisheries, and overall food security. Rising temperatures, erratic rainfall, and extreme weather events lead to significant yield loss in major food crops and economic instability among farmers. Climate-resilient agriculture has emerged as a sustainable approach to address these challenges by enhancing adaptive capacity, improving productivity, and reducing greenhouse gas emissions. This article highlights the impact of climate change on agriculture and discusses key adaptation strategies, extension services, and institutional support systems necessary for building resilience.

Keywords: Climate change, resilience, adaptation, food security, sustainable agriculture
